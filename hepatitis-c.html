Description

Hepatitis C is a liver infection caused by the hepatitis C virus (HCV). Hepatitis C can range from a mild illness lasting a few weeks to a serious, lifelong illness that can lead to cirrhosis and liver cancer.

Global Statistics

An estimated 58 million people have chronic hepatitis C infection globally, with approximately 1.5 million new infections occurring each year.

Symptoms

Acute HCV

  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Dark urine
  • Clay-colored stool
  • Abdominal pain

Chronic HCV

  • Often asymptomatic for years
  • Bleeding easily
  • Bruising easily
  • Jaundice
  • Fluid accumulation
